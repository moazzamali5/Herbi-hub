Plant Classification



Classifying living things depends on their similarities is useful for providing order to thousands of organisms on earth. With the aid of plant classification, professionals or any person related will be able to make appropriate determinations in controlling and managing plants. Meanwhile, proper naming of plants based on the classification are also significant to ease communication among professionals across the world instead of being confused by common names.



Taxonomy
is the science to identify, classify and name the variety of organisms


The classification system is arranged from largest to smallest:



  1. Kingdom
  2. Phylum or Division
  3. Class
  4. Order
  5. Family
  6. Genus
  7. Species


What is in a Botanical Name?



Commonly, naming of a plant is written according to its family, genus and species name (variety is included if applicable).




Thus, family, genus and species are especially important in plant classification. Scroll down to learn more!





Families



Family (plural: families) is an official botanical classification, usually referred as the highest classification group, by ended with “-aceae” in the naming. Plant families are differentiated based on their structural characteristics such as flowers, fruit and seed.


Example of Family


Dipterocarpaceae


  • consists of 22 genera and approximately 695 known species
  • distribution: from northern South America to Africa, the Seychelles, India, Indochina, Indonesia, Malaysia and Philippines
  • size: majority are large forest-emergent species, achieving heights of 40-70m, some even exceed 80 m
  • supply valuable woods, aromatic essential oils, balsam, resins and source for plywood

Genus



Genus (plural: genera) refers to the plants under the same family classification that hold more similar characteristics with one another than other plants which are also within the family. In naming, genus is the first part of the scientific or Latin name for plants. Basically, the first letter of the genus name is capitalized and italicized or underlined:

  • Quercus
  • Quercus

Example of Genus


Hopea


  • consists of approximately 113 species
  • distribution: from Sri Lanka and southern India to the Andaman Islands, Myanmar, southern China, and southward throughout Malesia to New Guinea
  • size: small or medium, however can grow irregularly large
  • low branches, thick buttress roots, chocolate brown bark and smooth initially, then crack and mottle with grey spots
  • extinct in East Himalaya

Species



Species is the level of classification that defines specific plant. Some aspects used to define the plants include their colour, leaf shape and place where or by whom it was explored. There are approximately 380 000 known species of plants. The genus and specific epithet are matched to give the naming for the species.

Example of Species


Hopea odorata Roxb.


  • common name: Chengal Pasir, Merawan Siput Jantan, Chengal Kampong, Cengal Pasir, Cengal Pulau, Telshur, Takhian-yai, Ironwood
  • distribution: Bangladesh, Myanmar, Laos, southern Vietnam, Cambodia, Thailand, the Andaman Islands and northern Peninsular Malaysia
  • habitat: majority located near streams ang rivers
  • size: medium to large, height of 25-30m, sometimes achieve 40m
  • straight trunk, short buttresses, dark brownish-grey outer bark and dull yellow inner bark
  • flower
    • yellow or golden colour
    • located in leaf axils or branch tips
    • fragrant
  • fruit
    • brown winged nut
    • approximately 6x5 mm
    • have 2 long wings and 3 short wings, which aid in seed dispersal by wind
